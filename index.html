What is AI?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the ability of software to learn from data, recognise patterns, and automate tasks.

Although AI in recent years returned to everyday conversation - thanks to Large Language Models (LLMs) such as OpenAI's ChatGPT, X's Grok, and Microsoft Copilot - it has been around for decades. You may already be using AI without realising it. If your business uses Search Engine Optimisation (SEO), or if you receive personalised recommendations on platforms like Netflix, YouTube, or social media, or use virtual assistants such as Google Assistant, Siri, or Alexa — you're already using AI.

For small businesses, AI isn't about robots; it's about practical tools that save time, reduce mistakes, and free people to focus on what matters most. Without realising it, you might already be paying for tools that can streamline your workflow and optimise your customer service.

AI is already built into many of the tools small businesses use every day. Platforms like Canva can automatically enhance images and suggest layouts, Xero and QuickBooks use AI to categorise expenses, and tools like Grammarly and ChatGPT help polish communication and generate content. Even scheduling apps, email marketing services, and spam filters rely on AI to make smart decisions in the background.

There are automatisation tools that let small business owners connect their everyday apps — like Gmail, Xero, and Google Sheets, and build simple workflows without any coding. These tools aren't technically "AI," but they often go hand-in-hand with AI systems, helping you cut down on repetitive admin and free up time for real work.
